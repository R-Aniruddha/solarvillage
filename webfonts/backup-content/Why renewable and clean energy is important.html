Why renewable and clean energy is important?

The electricity supply across India lacks quantity and quality due to shortage of supply and transmission and distribution losses because of theft and lack of proper infrastructure. Rural India is primarily affected by this energy shortage. Thus it is common for villages to have long hours of blackouts everyday. This forces rural population to rely on polluting energy sources like firewood, agricultural wastes, animal wastes, and kerosene or diesel generators. These sources severely contribute towards climate change and affects human health. Irregular electricity supply also constrains economic development and affects overall quality of life due to limited access to basic necessities like water, education and hygiene.

Increasing use of polluting sources for energy generation is contributing towards climate change. According to the United Nations, energy is the dominant contributor towards climate change, accounting for around 60% of total global greenhouse gas emissions. Thus, addressing these energy concerns are important in order to increase India’s electricity generation capacity. It is also important to consider the means for increasing such generation and therefore increasing the share of renewable sources. India is blessed with abundance solar energy and Auroville Consulting aims to install Solar PV Systems in 100 Villages of Tamil Nadu.

Economic Opportunities

Reliable and continuous power supply allows local manufacturing units and business operations to operate longer and increase their productivity. This leads to increase in income and reduces rural to urban migration in search of work with higher pay.

Quality of Life

Providing better municipal services such as reliable water supply, sanitation, street lighting and education depends on the supply of electricity. 24 x 7 supply of electricity will substantially impact quality of life of the rural population in a positive way.

Top-aligned media

To overcome energy problems and combating carbon emission, climate change, harnessing clean and renewable sources is thus essential.

Improved Education

Provision of lighting enables longer study hours allowing children to study in evenings, leading to better educational outcomes.

Reduces Health Concerns

Switching to electricity for lighting, cooking and heating means eliminating the indoor air pollution caused by the smoke emitted by kerosene lamps, firewood and other polluting energy sources. The smoke emitted from these sources is harmful for human health causing various diseases.

Solar Village Initiative

The Solar Village Initiative aims to provide 24x7 equal access to clean, reliable and renewable electricity to the villages in order to achieve sustainable rural development. We aim to increase the share of renewable energy thus reducing the carbon intensity and GHG emission caused by burning fossil fuels and achieve long-term sustainable development goals. In addition with this initiative we plan to shift the existing centralized ownership model in energy industry to a democratic model by transferring ownership to villagers and bring more transparency.

For each megawatt of Solar PV installed, a revolving fund will increase the installed capacity by a factor of 4 over 18 years. The project objective of powering 100 villages would lead to the socio-economic development and well- being of 1.2 lakh rural citizens of India through sustainable methods. For meeting our target of 100 villages, funds for 25 megawatts of Solar PV systems is required from the Solar Village search engine, Foundation grants and CSR budgets.

WHAT

For each Megawatt of solar PV installed a revolving fund will increase installed capacity by a factor of 4 over its lifetime. Which means an additional 3 MWp of solar PV will be installed. Ultimately we aim at powering 100 villages

Why

The objective here is to provide 24x7 equal access to clean and renewable energy to the people in these villages and thereby contribute to the sustainable rural development and democratization of ownership model

Where

The solar village project proposes to install solar PV systems in rural areas of the Viluppuram district of Tamil Nadu. Viluppuram is one of the most backward districts of Tamil Nadu and has 1,431 villages with population sizes varying from below 200 to above 10,000 inhabitants.

  • Improved quality of life of the people
  • Transfer of ownership after 17 years
  • Revenue generation for village Panchayat and/or farmers through land lease
  • Increased working hours leading to economic development
  • A part of the project revenue used for promoting sports, education in the village
  • Less environmental and health concerns related to energy use
  • Local employment generation
  • Improved level of education
Costing

Solarizing one village with a population size of ~1,200 will require 1 MWp solar PV capacity. This includes electricity consumption for agriculture and an estimated annual electric energy increase of 8% over the next five years.

Concept

Over its lifetime, every installed 1 MWp solar PV system will generate revenue through sales of power that will allow installation of 3 MWp additional solar PV systems. This is an increase by a factor of 4. In addition to that, 2% of the annual revenue generated through sales of power will be disbursed to the village Panchayat in cash and another 2% in kind to cover electricity expenses of municipal services such as water pumps and streetlights.

Implimentation

Auroville Consulting will be responsible for overall project management and implementation. Ownership of the Solar PV power plant will be transferred to the villagers after 18 years. A power purchase agreement will be signed with the utility provider or a private company, as found suitable.
